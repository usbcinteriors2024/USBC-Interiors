FAQ


1. What’s your Interior Design budget? +

The first question that is posed is this: some designers are reluctant to discuss their budget up front, but why waste everyone's time if their budget isn't in line with the goods and services you offer? At the very least, you need to ascertain that their request falls within a reasonable budget "range" that will enable you to grant it.
One of the most important questions you can ask your client is this one. Your ability to create and the materials you can utilize will depend on how much money you have to spend.

2. What is your Interior Design timeframe?+

When a customer claims that they aren't in a rush and that they want these 6 rooms finished by the holidays—which are in 8 weeks—it is possible that they have an incorrect perception of how long a well-executed design project takes. Be sure you aren't making unrealistic time promises. Every time you do that, a dissatisfied client will result.

3. Have you worked with an Interior design companies before?+

Why are you switching designers. If a client has a lot of negative things to say about a previous interior designer, chances are good that they will soon have the same problems with you. Recognize these warning signs while you still have the option to leave the job.

5. What does an interior designer in Dubai actually do? +

Depending on their area of expertise, interior designers in Dubai may undertake a variety of tasks. While some interior designers specialize in business or hospitality design, others may concentrate on domestic design. The duties of an interior designer include space planning, furniture selection, color and material selection, lighting design, and lighting installation. They might collaborate with Interior Design Contractors to ensure that a project is finished on schedule and under budget, and they might be involved in branding and promoting it.

6. What is the difference between interior designers and interior decorators? +

Interior designers are skilled professionals who have finished a formal degree in design, whereas interior decorators are typically not needed to have any official training. This is the major distinction between interior designers and interior decorators. The layout and purpose of a space are often determined in collaboration with clients, after which the right furniture and materials are chosen to create the intended style. On the other hand, interior decorators typically concentrate on choosing and arranging furnishings, accessories, and other decor for a space. Nonetheless, there are numerous duties and responsibilities that designers and decorators share, making it difficult to distinguish clearly between the two professions.
