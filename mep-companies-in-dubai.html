Certifications and Licenses

Certifications and licenses are crucial for MEP companies in Dubai to operate legally and ensure that they meet industry standards. Here are some of the certifications and licenses that an MEP company in Dubai may need:

Trade License: Every company in Dubai is required to have a trade license issued by the Department of Economic Development (DED).

Dubai Municipality License: An MEP company must obtain a license from the Dubai Municipality to operate within the city.

Dubai Civil Defence License: MEP companies that provide fire protection and safety services need to obtain a license from the Dubai Civil Defence.

Dubai Electricity and Water Authority (DEWA) License: MEP companies that work with electrical and plumbing systems need to obtain a license from DEWA.

Dubai Silicon Oasis Authority (DSOA) License: MEP companies operating within the Dubai Silicon Oasis need to obtain a license from DSOA.

ISO Certification: An MEP company can obtain an ISO certification to demonstrate their compliance with international standards for quality management, environmental management, and occupational health and safety.

NEBOSH Certification: MEP companies can obtain NEBOSH (National Examination Board in Occupational Safety and Health) certification for their employees to ensure that they have the necessary knowledge and skills to manage health and safety risks.

Dubai Central Laboratory (DCL) Certification: MEP companies that provide testing and calibration services can obtain certification from DCL to demonstrate their compliance with international standards.

LEED Certification: MEP companies can obtain Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design (LEED) certification to demonstrate their commitment to sustainable building practices.

Dubai Green Building Regulations and Specifications (DGBRS): An MEP company must comply with GBS guidelines to ensure that their projects meet the environmental and sustainability standards set by the Dubai Municipality.
